University of Regina

Master of Science in Computer Science - Data Science (MSc Data Science Course Route) Program

Offered at Department of Computer Science

Program Overview

The Master of Data Science (MSc Data Science) program is designed to equip students with advanced knowledge and practical skills in data analysis, machine learning, artificial intelligence, and data-driven decision-making. The program emphasizes interdisciplinary learning, hands-on experience, and strong industry connections to ensure graduates are well-prepared to tackle complex data challenges in various sectors.

Program Duration: Full-Time: 28-32 months | Part-Time: Up to 40 months

Program Structure

Component Credits
Core Curriculum 24
Capstone Project 6
Internship/Co-op 0

Core Curriculum

Python & Data Fundamentals
(3 Credits)

Develop advanced programming skills in Python and R, focusing on data manipulation, software development best practices, and coding efficiency.

Foundations of Data Science
(3 Credits)

An introductory course covering the entire data science lifecycle, emphasizing data ethics, effective data management practices, and the critical role of data in decision-making.

Statistical Methods for Data Science
(3 Credits)

In-depth exploration of advanced statistical techniques, hypothesis testing, and probabilistic models, equipping students with the skills to analyze and interpret complex data.

Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence
(3 Credits)

Dive into supervised and unsupervised learning, neural networks, and deep learning methodologies, with a focus on their applications in real-world scenarios.

Data Engineering and Big Data Technologies
(3 Credits)

Learn about distributed computing, cloud platforms, data warehousing, and popular big data frameworks to effectively handle large-scale datasets.

Optimization and Combinatorics for Data Science
(3 Credits)

Study mathematical optimization techniques and combinatorial algorithms essential for solving complex data-related problems.

Data Visualization and Communication
(3 Credits)

Master the art of data storytelling, utilizing visualization tools to effectively communicate insights and findings to diverse audiences.

Business Analytics and Technical Entrepreneurship
(3 Credits)

Explore the intersection of data science and business, applying analytical methods to entrepreneurial strategies and decision-making processes.

Elective Courses (Choose Credits)

  Students can personalize their learning experience by selecting electives that align with their interests and career aspirations. Example electives include:

  • Deep Learning and Neural Networks: Advanced techniques for developing intelligent systems.
  • Natural Language Processing: Understanding and processing human language data.
  • Reinforcement Learning: Learning through interaction and feedback.
  • Time Series Analysis and Forecasting: Techniques for analyzing temporal data.
  • Advanced Data Visualization Techniques: Cutting-edge tools and methods for impactful visualizations.
  • Ethics in AI and Data Science: Examining the ethical implications of data use and AI technologies.
  • Statistical Consulting: Practical experience in consulting and applying statistical methods.
  • Experimental Design and Causal Inference: Designing experiments to establish cause-and-effect relationships.
  • IoT and Data Science: Leveraging data from the Internet of Things for innovative solutions.

Capstone Project (6 Credits)

A comprehensive project that allows students to apply their knowledge to real-world data science problems. Projects can be undertaken in collaboration with industry partners, ensuring practical relevance and exposure to current industry challenges.

Professional Experience (0 Credits)

  • Internship/Co-op (0 Credits):

    A mandatory 4-8 month industry internship or co-op placement, providing hands-on experience and networking opportunities.

  • Alternative Option: Research Thesis (0 Credits):

    For students interested in academic or research careers, a supervised research project can be undertaken as an alternative to the internship.

Unique Program Features

  1. Interdisciplinary Curriculum: Combines computer science, statistics, mathematics, and business analytics for a well-rounded education.
  2. Mandatory Professional Experience: Co-op or internship component ensures practical industry experience before graduation.
  3. Strong Emphasis on Artificial Intelligence: Deep integration of AI and machine learning into the curriculum, reflecting their growing importance.
  4. Mathematical Rigor: Advanced courses in optimization and combinatorics build strong analytical foundations.
  5. Flexibility and Customization: A wide range of electives allows specialization in areas like deep learning, NLP, or data engineering.
  6. Capstone Project with Industry Collaboration: Projects aligned with current industry needs, offering networking opportunities.
  7. Global Accessibility and Online Components: Online modules or blended learning options attract a diverse, global student body.
  8. Entrepreneurial and Communication Skills: Courses on technical entrepreneurship and data communication prepare students for leadership roles.
  9. Strong Industry Partnerships: Collaborations with tech companies and research institutions provide internships, guest lectures, and project collaborations.
  10. Cutting-Edge Research Opportunities: Access to research centers encourages engagement in innovative projects.
  11. Comprehensive Support Services: Career counseling, mentorship, and networking events support student career development.

Program Outcomes

  • Effectively communicate data-driven insights to diverse audiences.
  • Gain practical industry experience through internships or co-ops.
  • Be prepared for leadership roles in data science, analytics, and AI-driven organizations.

Conclusion
